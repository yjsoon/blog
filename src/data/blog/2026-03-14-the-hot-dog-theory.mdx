Actually there's one more perspective I'd like to offer, to balance out my own AI-maximalist tendencies. I came across this video with an amazing analogy about how AI coding feels like you're making hot dogs — processed, nice-looking, no idea what's inside — where previously you were making artisanal hand-crafted meals.

<YouTubeEmbed videoId="pzkwn3hu1Cc" title="I was a 10x engineer. Now I'm useless." />

And I actually get that kind of feeling because when I vibe code something, I no longer feel... anything about it. I don't feel like telling everyone about it and to download it, I no longer feel happy that I made this thing exist; its value to me is just "OK I made it now I can use it". Which could be a problem for the students — or is it just a problem because I've been through the "struggle" of hand-coding?

There's a good counterpoint here: [Vibe Coding — The Fast Food of Coding](https://dev.to/this-is-learning/vibe-coding-the-fast-food-of-coding-211o) from the DEV community. The framing is a bit different — it's less about the emotional loss and more about the practical risks — but it gets at the same tension.

Not sure yet how this plays out for my students. The hot dog vs artisanal meal thing is a nice metaphor, but I'm still figuring out whether the loss of struggle means the loss of something essential, or if it's just... different. Maybe the pride shifts from "I built this" to "I decided this"? Still thinking out loud here.
